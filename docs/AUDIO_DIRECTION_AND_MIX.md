# Audio Direction and Mix Strategy

Status: production plan ready; generation blocked until local ElevenLabs credentials and Zach Goodbody's signed voice consent are present.

## Musical identity

The score should grow with the factory rather than behave like a playlist:

- **Garage:** intimate analog synth, restrained guitar, light metallic percussion, human scale.
- **Job shop:** stronger rhythmic systems and mechanical accents as scheduling and throughput emerge.
- **Precision challenges:** sparse pulse and controlled tension; music must leave room for tool and inspection feedback.
- **Expansion:** broader drums and harmonic width, earned through gameplay rather than constant triumph.
- **Industrial titan:** large but disciplined sound—modern manufacturing confidence, not military bombast.

Every cue is instrumental, original, free of artist/song references, and intended for loop editing after generation. Music should duck 6–9 dB during Zach dialogue and 3 dB during critical machine alarms or task feedback.

## Zach Goodbody voice direction

- Plainspoken American shop-floor mentor; calm authority, practical warmth, no announcer voice.
- Medium pace with short pauses around technical instructions.
- CNC, G-code, alloy, inspection, and metrology terms must remain precise.
- Zach advises; he never claims ownership of the player's company or performs the player's accomplishments.
- Release only human-reviewed takes. Captions must remain authoritative even when timing is later aligned to audio.

## SFX layers

1. **Persistent ambience:** small-shop and large-factory loops, kept quiet enough to avoid fatigue.
2. **Equipment loops:** CNC mill and lathe cycles start and stop with station state.
3. **Machine one-shots:** doors, tool changes, saw cuts, pallet delivery.
4. **Precision foley:** caliper and micrometer confirmations reinforce real process steps.
5. **UI feedback:** quality pass/fail, coins/XP, quests, and facility unlocks use a consistent machined-metal palette.

Never use SFX as a substitute for visible safety state. Provide independent music, voice, and SFX volume controls; captions default on; do not encode essential information in sound alone.

## Review and mastering targets

- Remove hallucinated voices, melodies, alarms, unsafe machine behavior, clipping, and unusable loop seams.
- Normalize dialogue consistently and keep true peaks below -1 dBTP.
- Establish loop points in lossless masters before delivery compression.
- Retain generation receipts, prompt IDs, and human approval status.
- Test laptop speakers, headphones, phone speakers, and muted play.
